## üìä Key Data Points from CSTEP Research

### **India's APV Potential:**
- **Minimum:** 2,800 GW (2.8 TW)
- **Maximum:** 13,803 GW (13.8 TW)
- Varies across different technologies and crops
- Assessed for 17 selected crop varieties

### **Technical Specifications:**
- **Module Elevation:** 5 meters clearance, up to 7.8m total height
- **Unit Width:** 19 meters
- **Foundation:** Corkscrew bolts (spider anchors) - no concrete
- **Configurations:** 3 main types (overhead, inter-row, vertical)

### **Crop Performance:**
- **Shade-loving crops:** Improved yields (leafy greens, tomatoes, root vegetables)
- **Traditional crops:** May see yield reduction (rice, wheat, oilseeds)
- **Regulatory Standard:** Maximum 33% yield reduction allowed (German standard, India developing own)

### **Environmental Benefits:**
- Reduced evapotranspiration ‚Üí 90% water savings
- Improved PV efficiency through natural cooling
- Protection from extreme weather (heat, drought, hail)
- Enhanced biodiversity and ecosystem health
- Better soil quality and water regulation

### **Economic Benefits:**
- Diversified farmer income (crops + electricity)
- Farmers become "energy entrepreneurs"
- Rural job creation (maintenance, operation, crop production)
- Strengthened rural economies
- Support through PM-KUSUM scheme (90% subsidy)
